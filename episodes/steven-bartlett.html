About Steven Bartlett: Steven Bartlett is the host of The Diary of a CEO, one of the world's most popular podcasts. He is a British-Nigerian entrepreneur who founded the social media company Social Chain at age 21, which he took public at age 27. He is also the youngest ever investor on BBC's Dragons' Den and the author of 'The Diary of a CEO: The 33 Laws of Business and Life'.

In this rare solo episode, Steven Bartlett opens up about the 33 laws he has distilled from years of building businesses, conducting thousands of interviews, and navigating personal struggles. He shares the principles from his bestselling book in a deeply personal format, revealing the stories behind each law.

Steven begins with Law 1: "Fill your five buckets in the right order" ‚Äî knowledge, skills, network, resources, and reputation. He explains why most people focus on money (resources) first when it should actually be the fourth priority. The first investment should always be in learning.

The conversation covers some of the most powerful laws including "You must out-fail the competition," "You must lean into bizarre behavior," and "You must never compromise your self-story." Steven shares vulnerable stories about his childhood in Plymouth, growing up in poverty, his mother's struggles, and how those early experiences shaped his relentless drive.

Perhaps the most powerful segment is Steven's discussion of the importance of asking questions you're afraid to hear the answers to ‚Äî in business, in relationships, and in life. He argues that the quality of your life is directly proportional to the quality of questions you're willing to ask and the discomfort you're willing to endure.
