Ramit Sethi: Money Expert: The Hidden Reason Why You're Broke & Can't Get Rich!

About Ramit Sethi: Ramit Sethi is the bestselling author of 'I Will Teach You To Be Rich' and the host of the Netflix series 'How to Get Rich'. He is a Stanford graduate who has built a multi-million dollar personal finance education company. Ramit is known for his contrarian approach to money, challenging conventional wisdom about homeownership, frugality, and wealth building.

In this eye-opening episode, Ramit Sethi challenges everything you think you know about money and reveals the hidden psychological reasons why most people stay broke despite earning decent incomes. His contrarian approach to personal finance has made him one of the most polarizing ‚Äî and effective ‚Äî money experts in the world.

Ramit starts with a bombshell: he argues that buying a house is often the worst financial decision people make. He presents data showing that renting and investing the difference outperforms homeownership in most scenarios, and that the emotional attachment to owning property prevents people from making rational financial decisions.

The conversation covers Ramit's concept of "money dials" ‚Äî the idea that instead of cutting back on everything, you should identify the 2-3 areas where spending brings you genuine joy and spend extravagantly on those while ruthlessly cutting costs on everything else. He argues that the latte-cutting, coupon-clipping approach to personal finance is both psychologically damaging and financially insignificant.

Steven and Ramit also discuss the invisible scripts around money that people inherit from their parents, why couples fight about money, and why having a "rich life" has nothing to do with the amount in your bank account and everything to do with intentional choices about what matters most to you.
