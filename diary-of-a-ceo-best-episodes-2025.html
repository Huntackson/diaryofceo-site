The Top 10 Diary of a CEO Episodes in 2025

1. Dr. Andrew Huberman — "The Sleep Protocol That Changes Everything"

Huberman's return to the show was electric. This wasn't a rehash of old advice — he brought new peer-reviewed research on sleep architecture and delivered a practical 7-day protocol that listeners could start immediately. The episode clocked over 15 million views in its first month and became the most-shared health episode in the show's history.

Key takeaway: Your first 90 minutes of sleep determine 80% of your recovery. Temperature, not duration, is the lever most people ignore.

2. Jensen Huang — "Why NVIDIA Bet Everything on AI (And Almost Lost)"

The NVIDIA CEO rarely does long-form interviews, making this a genuine coup. Huang was disarmingly honest about the years NVIDIA was mocked for its AI investments and the internal moments of doubt that nearly killed the strategy. A masterclass in conviction under uncertainty.

Key takeaway: "The market will tell you you're wrong for years before it tells you you're right. You have to know the difference between the market being wrong and you being wrong."

3. Dr. Gabor Maté — "The Trauma You Don't Know You're Carrying"

Maté's episode went deep — uncomfortably deep — into how childhood experiences wire adult behavior patterns. What made this episode exceptional was the live coaching segment where Bartlett himself opened up. Raw, real, and genuinely therapeutic for millions of listeners.

Key takeaway: Addiction isn't about the substance. It's about the pain the substance temporarily solves. Healing starts with asking "what happened to you?" not "what's wrong with you?"

4. Sam Altman — "The Next 5 Years of AI, Honestly"

In a world of AI hype, Altman was surprisingly measured. He acknowledged the jobs that will disappear, the ethical failures OpenAI has navigated, and his genuine fears. The most googled episode of Q1 2025.

Key takeaway: The people who will thrive aren't those who learn to use AI tools — they're those who learn to ask better questions than AI can generate on its own.

5. Alex Hormozi — "The $100M Framework for Any Business"

Hormozi stripped away the noise and delivered what might be the most practically useful business episode in podcast history. He walked through his actual acquisition framework, complete with the spreadsheet he uses to evaluate deals. Listeners reported implementing his advice within 48 hours.

Key takeaway: Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. But owner earnings — what you actually take home after reinvestment — is reality. Most entrepreneurs optimize the wrong number.

6. Brené Brown — "Why Vulnerability Is a Competitive Advantage"

Brown challenged the "hustle culture" narrative with research showing that leaders who practice vulnerability outperform those who project invulnerability by measurable margins. Her framework for "rumbling with tough conversations" gave managers a tool they could use Monday morning.

7. Chris Bumstead — "The Price of Being the Best in the World"

CBum's candid discussion about the physical and psychological toll of elite bodybuilding was haunting. He spoke openly about body dysmorphia, kidney health scares, and the moment he realized his identity had become entirely fused with his physique. A powerful episode about the hidden costs of excellence.

8. Dr. Mindy Pelz — "Fasting, Hormones, and What Your Doctor Won't Tell You"

This episode corrected widespread fasting misinformation — particularly for women. Dr. Pelz explained how fasting protocols need to sync with hormonal cycles, and why the "one size fits all" approach to intermittent fasting is potentially harmful. The most saved episode on Spotify in January 2025.

9. Daniel Kahneman — "The Thinking Mistakes That Ruin Your Life" (Posthumous Release)

Recorded before Kahneman's passing, this episode felt like a gift. The Nobel laureate's final long-form interview covered his latest thinking on noise, bias, and why humans remain stubbornly irrational despite knowing better. A legacy episode in every sense.

10. Bartlett Solo — "What I Learned Losing £10 Million"

Steven's solo episodes are always revealing, but this one was different. He detailed a specific investment failure, the ego that drove it, and the lessons he extracted. No vague platitudes — actual numbers, actual mistakes, actual growth. The comment section became a confessional for entrepreneurs sharing their own losses.

What Makes 2025 Different

Looking at the Diary of a CEO best episodes of 2025 as a whole, there's a clear shift: the show has moved from inspiration toward implementation. Earlier seasons asked "what do successful people believe?" The 2025 episodes ask "what do successful people do, specifically, and how can you do it too?"

That shift — from motivation to mechanism — is why the podcast continues to grow while others plateau. It's also why resources like diaryofceo.online have become essential companions, helping listeners track and apply what they learn across episodes.
