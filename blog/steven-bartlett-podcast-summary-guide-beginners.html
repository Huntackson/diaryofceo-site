What Is The Diary of a CEO?

The Diary of a CEO (often abbreviated as DOAC) is a long-form interview podcast hosted by Steven Bartlett. Launched in 2017, it has grown into one of the most popular podcasts in the world, consistently ranking #1 in the UK and top 10 globally on Spotify and Apple Podcasts.

What sets DOAC apart from other interview shows is its depth. While most podcasts rush through surface-level questions in 30-45 minutes, Bartlett typically spends 1.5 hours with each guest, creating space for genuine vulnerability and detailed storytelling. The result is conversations that feel more like private confessions than standard interviews.

Guests range from billionaire entrepreneurs and Hollywood A-listers to neuroscientists, psychologists, and world-class athletes. The common thread: every guest has achieved something extraordinary and has hard-won wisdom to share.

Who Is Steven Bartlett?

Steven Bartlett is a British-Nigerian entrepreneur who dropped out of university at 22 to build Social Chain, a social media marketing company he grew to a $200M+ valuation before stepping down as CEO. He became the youngest-ever investor on BBC's Dragons' Den and has built a personal brand as one of the most influential voices in entrepreneurship and personal development.

What makes Bartlett an exceptional interviewer is his own experience with failure, poverty, and self-doubt. He asks the questions most interviewers avoid ‚Äî about depression, financial struggles, relationship failures, and the unglamorous reality behind public success.

How the Podcast Works

Every DOAC episode follows a loose but effective structure:

  • Opening: Bartlett introduces the guest and sets context for why this conversation matters
  • Deep dive: The main conversation ‚Äî usually 60-90 minutes of uninterrupted discussion
  • The final question: Bartlett often asks guests to leave one piece of advice
  • Closing: A brief recap and call to action

Episodes are available on YouTube (with video), Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and all major podcast platforms. The YouTube versions often include visual aids, reactions, and are the most popular format.
