Best Business & Entrepreneurship Episodes (2025)

BUSINESS

Jensen Huang — "Why NVIDIA Bet Everything on AI"

The NVIDIA CEO's candid breakdown of betting the company on AI — years before the rest of the world caught on — is the single best business episode of 2025. Huang doesn't just talk strategy; he reveals the emotional toll of holding conviction when your board, your investors, and the market all think you're wrong. Required listening for any founder navigating uncertainty.

Best for: Founders, investors, anyone making contrarian bets

BUSINESS

Sara Blakely — "Spanx, Failure, and Building a Billion-Dollar Brand Alone"

Blakely's episode cuts through the typical founder mythology. She talks about the years of rejection, the nights she questioned everything, and the specific moment she realized Spanx could be worth a billion dollars. Her framework for "reframing rejection as redirection" has become one of the most-quoted ideas from the show this year.

Best for: Solo founders, bootstrappers, women in business

BUSINESS

Alex Hormozi — "The $100M Playbook for 2025"

Hormozi returned with arguably his most tactical episode yet. Instead of high-level principles, he walked through specific pricing models, lead generation strategies, and the exact metrics he watches to know if a business is healthy. Listeners reported implementing his "offer stack" framework and seeing results within weeks.

Best for: Business owners looking for immediate tactical improvements

Best Health & Science Episodes (2025)

HEALTH

Dr. Andrew Huberman — "The Sleep Protocol That Changes Everything"

Huberman's third appearance on the show was his best. He brought new peer-reviewed research on sleep architecture and delivered a 7-day protocol that listeners could start immediately. The episode clocked over 15 million views in its first month. His insight that temperature — not duration — is the most overlooked sleep lever changed the conversation for millions of people.

Best for: Anyone struggling with sleep, energy, or recovery

HEALTH

Dr. Chris van Tulleken — "Ultra-Processed Food Is Destroying Your Brain"

This episode terrified people in the best possible way. Van Tulleken presented research linking ultra-processed food not just to obesity, but to cognitive decline, anxiety, and depression. His "five-ingredient rule" — if a product has more than five ingredients, think twice — became the most practical takeaway of any health episode this year.

Best for: Anyone wanting to understand modern nutrition

HEALTH

Dr. Rangan Chatterjee — "The 4-Pillar Health Framework"

While other health episodes went deep on one topic, Chatterjee provided a genuinely holistic framework. His argument that movement, nutrition, sleep, and relaxation are not separate pillars but interconnected systems resonated deeply. The "micro-habit stacking" technique he shared gave overwhelmed listeners a starting point that didn't require a complete life overhaul.

Best for: People who feel overwhelmed by conflicting health advice

Best Mindset & Psychology Episodes (2025)

MINDSET

Dr. Julie Smith — "Why You Self-Sabotage (And How to Stop)"

Dr. Julie Smith's episode became the most-shared psychology episode of the year. She didn't just explain self-sabotage — she gave listeners a specific journaling framework to identify their personal patterns. The concept of "protective behaviours that outlive their usefulness" gave language to something millions of people experience but can't articulate.

Best for: Anyone stuck in repetitive patterns they can't explain

MINDSET

Mo Gawdat — "The Happiness Equation No One Teaches You"

The former Google X chief brought his engineering mindset to happiness, and it worked brilliantly. His equation — happiness equals reality minus expectations — sounds simple, but the hour he spent unpacking it revealed profound nuances. Particularly powerful was his segment on grief, where he discussed losing his son and finding meaning through radical acceptance.

Best for: Anyone searching for a practical framework for contentment

Best Relationship & Communication Episodes (2025)

RELATIONSHIPS

Esther Perel — "The Secret Language of Modern Relationships"

Perel's episode was the most emotionally intense of the year. She challenged Steven — and the audience — on the difference between being loved and feeling loved, and why so many people in committed relationships still feel desperately alone. Her concept of "erotic intelligence" went far beyond physical intimacy into how we maintain curiosity about people we think we already know.

Best for: Anyone in a long-term relationship, or trying to understand why past ones failed

RELATIONSHIPS

Matthew Hussey — "The Communication Hack That Saves Relationships"

Hussey's return focused on a single, powerful idea: most relationship problems are communication problems in disguise. His "mirror and validate" technique — repeating what your partner said before responding — sounds almost too simple, but the research he cited showed it reduces conflict escalation by over 60%. Practical, immediate, and genuinely useful.

Best for: Couples who argue about the same things repeatedly
