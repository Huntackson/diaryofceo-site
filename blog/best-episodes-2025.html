Diary of a CEO Best Episodes 2025: The 12 Must-Listen Conversations That Defined the Year

Over 50 episodes. Hundreds of hours. We ranked them all so you can skip straight to the ones that will actually change how you think, work, and live.

2025 was arguably the biggest year yet for The Diary of a CEO. Steven Bartlett didn't just interview famous people — he pulled insights out of world-class scientists, billionaire entrepreneurs, elite athletes, and cultural icons that left listeners rethinking everything from their morning routines to their life goals.

But here's the problem: with each episode running around 1.5 hours, catching up on an entire year means committing 75+ hours. Most people don't have that. That's why we built diaryofceo.online — and why we've compiled this definitive list of the Diary of a CEO best episodes 2025.

These aren't ranked by views or celebrity status. They're ranked by lasting impact — the episodes listeners keep coming back to, sharing with friends, and referencing months later.

1. Chris Williamson — The Masculinity Episode That Broke the Internet

Chris Williamson's return to DOAC in 2025 was one of the most-discussed podcast episodes of the entire year — across any show. The conversation tackled modern masculinity, dating dynamics, and why young men are struggling, without falling into the usual political traps.

"The opposite of toxic masculinity isn't no masculinity. It's mature masculinity. And we've done a terrible job of showing young men what that looks like." — Chris Williamson on The Diary of a CEO

What made this a standout among the Diary of a CEO best episodes 2025 was Steven's willingness to push back. The result was a nuanced, 1.5-hour conversation that both sides of the debate found valuable — a rare achievement in today's media landscape.

2. Dr. Andrew Huberman — The Neuroscience of Motivation

Huberman's DOAC appearances are always blockbusters, but his 2025 episode went beyond the usual dopamine talking points. The conversation zeroed in on the neuroscience behind why we lose motivation — and the surprisingly simple protocols that restore it.

"Motivation isn't something you find. It's something your nervous system generates in response to very specific conditions. Change the conditions, change the motivation." — Dr. Andrew Huberman on The Diary of a CEO

The practical protocols shared in this episode — from morning sunlight exposure timing to the "gap effects" technique for learning retention — made it one of the most actionable episodes of the year.

3. Alex Hormozi — The $100M Playbook, Updated

Alex Hormozi came on DOAC in 2025 with updated data from his portfolio of companies, and the business lessons were staggering. The episode covered pricing psychology, offer creation, and why most businesses fail not because of bad products but because of bad framing.

Key insight: Hormozi shared his "value equation" framework — that perceived value equals dream outcome × perceived likelihood of achievement, divided by time delay × effort and sacrifice. Every business decision should move at least one of those four levers.

For entrepreneurs, this was the single most valuable episode of the year. The frameworks are immediately applicable to any business at any stage.

4. Dr. Rangan Chatterjee — Why Everything You Know About Stress Is Wrong

Dr. Chatterjee's 2025 DOAC appearance challenged the prevailing narrative that stress is the enemy. Drawing on new research, he argued that our relationship with stress — not stress itself — determines whether it helps or harms us.

"Stress isn't killing you. Your belief that stress is killing you — that's what's doing the damage. The research on this is now overwhelming." — Dr. Rangan Chatterjee on The Diary of a CEO

The episode included a practical "stress reframing" exercise that listeners could apply immediately, making it one of the most shared Diary of a CEO best episodes 2025 on social media.

5. Mo Gawdat — AI, Consciousness, and the Future of Humanity

Mo Gawdat's previous DOAC appearances made waves, but his 2025 conversation was different. With AI advancing faster than even experts predicted, Gawdat laid out a sobering but ultimately hopeful vision of what's coming — and what individuals can do to prepare.

The conversation covered everything from AI's impact on employment to the philosophical question of machine consciousness. Gawdat's ability to make complex topics accessible is unmatched, and Steven's questions pushed the conversation into territory most interviewers avoid.

6. Simon Sinek — The Leadership Crisis No One Is Talking About

Sinek's 2025 episode focused on a theme he's been building toward for years: the collapse of trust in institutions and what it means for leadership at every level. The conversation went beyond corporate boardrooms into how anyone — parents, teachers, team leads — can rebuild trust in their sphere of influence.

"We don't have a leadership crisis. We have a courage crisis. Leaders know what the right thing is. They're just terrified of the short-term cost of doing it." — Simon Sinek on The Diary of a CEO

7. Sahil Bloom — Building Wealth in Your 20s and 30s

Sahil Bloom's episode was a masterclass in personal finance and career strategy for younger listeners. Unlike most financial advice episodes, Bloom focused on the decisions that compound — career moves, relationship investments, and skill-stacking — rather than just investment vehicles.

8. Dr. Mindy Pelz — Fasting, Hormones, and the Gender Gap in Health Advice

Dr. Pelz's episode exposed a critical blind spot in health and wellness: most fasting and nutrition research has been conducted primarily on men. Her conversation with Steven about how women need fundamentally different protocols was eye-opening and immediately practical.
